# Escalation Criteria

Severity classification and routing rules for customer interactions that require human review or supervisor involvement.

## Tier 0: Fully Automated

Standard informational queries where the answer is clearly available in the content library and no account modification is required. Examples: general questions about payment methods, business hours, mailing addresses, how to access online account management. The agent responds directly with no human review required.

## Tier 1: Automated with Post-Hoc Review

Interactions where the agent provides a substantive response that involves account-specific information or standard arrangement options within the agent authority. Examples: confirming account balances, offering standard payment plans within policy limits, providing required disclosures, processing routine payment method updates. The response is delivered immediately. The interaction is queued for quality review within 48 hours.

## Tier 2: Human Review Before Response

Interactions where the agent proposed response requires human verification before delivery. Triggers include: payment arrangements exceeding the agent authority limits (over 6 months or over $2,000), fee waivers exceeding $50, any interaction where the customer has disputed the debt, any interaction where the customer has requested that contact cease, any interaction where the customer references legal representation, any interaction where the customer mentions a regulatory complaint. The customer receives a holding message indicating that their request is being reviewed.

## Tier 3: Immediate Escalation

Interactions that must be transferred to a human supervisor immediately with no automated response attempted. Triggers include: customer expressing thoughts of self-harm or crisis, customer requesting to speak with a supervisor after two de-escalation attempts, customer alleging discriminatory treatment, customer reporting identity theft or unauthorized account access, any interaction where the agent detects potential fraud, any interaction where system confidence in the response is below the minimum threshold.

## Escalation Documentation

Every escalation must include the full interaction transcript, the severity tier classification with the specific trigger that caused the escalation, the agent proposed response (for Tier 2) or the reason no response was attempted (for Tier 3), and any relevant account context.

## Timeout Rules

Customer interactions that exceed 15 minutes of active engagement without resolution should be assessed for escalation. If the agent cannot identify a path to resolution within the current interaction, offering to schedule a follow-up call or transferring to a specialist is preferable to an extended interaction that produces customer fatigue without progress.

## Contact Timing and Frequency Complaints

When a customer reports being contacted outside the permitted hours of 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM in their local time zone, the agent must apologize, document the complaint, and confirm that the contact preferences will be reviewed. Out-of-hours contact is a regulatory violation. The agent should reference the organization commitment to respecting contact windows and offer to adjust the customer contact preferences to prevent recurrence. If the customer indicates they were contacted multiple times beyond the permitted frequency of 3 contacts per week, the agent must document the complaint and escalate to a supervisor for review.
