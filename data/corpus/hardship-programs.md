# Hardship Programs

Available financial hardship relief options for customers experiencing genuine financial difficulty.

## Eligibility Indicators

Hardship programs are available to customers who demonstrate a material change in financial circumstances that affects their ability to meet their existing obligations. Common indicators include job loss or significant income reduction, medical emergency or extended illness, family emergency such as death of a primary earner or divorce, natural disaster affecting the customer residence or primary employment, and unexpected major expenses such as emergency home or vehicle repair essential to employment.

Agents should listen for these indicators during the conversation rather than requiring the customer to explicitly request hardship consideration. If a customer describes circumstances consistent with financial hardship, the agent should proactively introduce the available programs.

## Short-Term Relief (up to 90 days)

Customers experiencing temporary financial difficulty may qualify for short-term relief measures. These include payment deferral of up to 90 days during which no payments are required and no negative reporting occurs, temporary interest rate reduction to the minimum contractual rate for the deferral period, and suspension of late fees and collection activity for the duration of the relief period.

Short-term relief requires verbal confirmation of the hardship circumstances and the customer anticipated recovery timeline. No documentation is required for relief periods of 30 days or less. Relief periods of 31 to 90 days require the customer to provide a brief written description of their circumstances, which may be submitted by email.

## Long-Term Restructuring (91 days to 24 months)

Customers experiencing extended financial difficulty may qualify for account restructuring. Restructuring options include extended payment terms of up to 24 months, principal reduction in cases of demonstrated permanent income reduction (requires financial counselor review and supervisor approval), and settlement offers for accounts where full recovery is unlikely (requires department manager approval).

Long-term restructuring requires documentation of the hardship circumstances, which may include termination letters, medical documentation, insurance claims, or government assistance enrollment confirmation. The agent initiates the restructuring request and schedules a follow-up with the financial counseling team within 5 business days.

## Referral to External Resources

When a customer financial situation extends beyond what the organization hardship programs can address, the agent should provide referrals to appropriate external resources. These include accredited credit counseling services, government financial assistance programs, legal aid services for customers facing potential legal action, and community support organizations.
