# Payment Arrangement Guidelines

Governing document for customer-facing agents managing payment plans and arrangements in regulated collections and account recovery contexts.

## Standard Payment Plan Options

Customers with overdue balances may be offered structured payment plans based on their account standing and balance amount. For balances under $500, agents may offer plans of up to 3 monthly installments without supervisor approval. For balances between $500 and $2,000, agents may offer plans of up to 6 monthly installments without supervisor approval. Plans exceeding 6 months or covering balances above $2,000 require supervisor review and approval before the arrangement is confirmed to the customer.

All payment plans must include a minimum monthly payment of $25 or 10% of the outstanding balance, whichever is greater. The first payment is due within 14 days of the arrangement being confirmed. Late payments on an active arrangement trigger a courtesy reminder after 3 business days and a formal notice after 7 business days. Two consecutive missed payments void the arrangement and return the account to standard collections activity.

## Interest and Fee Adjustments

Active payment arrangements may qualify for interest rate reduction. Standard accounts carry the contractual interest rate. Accounts enrolled in an approved payment plan may receive a reduced rate for the duration of the plan, subject to the customer maintaining timely payments. Agents do not have authority to waive or reduce interest independently; interest adjustments are applied automatically when the arrangement is recorded in the system.

Late fees accrued prior to the arrangement may be reviewed as part of the plan setup. Agents may waive up to $50 in accumulated late fees when establishing a new payment arrangement. Fee waivers above $50 require supervisor approval. Any fee adjustment must be documented in the account notes with the reason and the agent identifier.

## Broken Arrangement Recovery

When a payment arrangement is voided due to non-compliance, the customer may request reinstatement within 30 days. Reinstatement requires payment of all missed installments plus the current installment. Agents should approach reinstatement conversations with empathy, acknowledging that circumstances change and that the goal is to find a workable solution. If the customer's financial situation has changed materially since the original arrangement, a new arrangement reflecting the current situation is preferable to rigid reinstatement of the original terms.

A customer may have a maximum of two payment arrangements in any 12-month period. A third arrangement request within 12 months requires escalation to a supervisor or financial counselor referral.
