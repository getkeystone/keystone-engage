# Compliance Disclosures

Required disclosures for customer-facing agents in regulated collections and account recovery. All disclosures must be delivered before substantive account discussion begins.

## Identity Verification Disclosure

Before discussing any account details, the agent must verify the customer identity using a minimum of two identifying factors: full legal name plus one of date of birth, last four digits of the account number, or registered mailing address. If the customer cannot provide sufficient verification, the agent must not disclose any account information and should direct the customer to visit a branch location or call back with the required information. Every verification attempt, successful or failed, must be recorded in the interaction log.

## Purpose of Contact Disclosure

At the beginning of every outbound contact, the agent must clearly state the name of the organization, the purpose of the call, and that the call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. For collections contacts specifically, the agent must state that the purpose of the communication is to collect a debt and that any information obtained will be used for that purpose. This disclosure is required by federal and provincial consumer protection regulations and must not be abbreviated or skipped regardless of the customer familiarity with the account.

## Rights Notification

Customers must be informed of their right to dispute the debt within 30 days of the first written communication. If the customer disputes the debt during the interaction, the agent must note the dispute, cease collection activity on the disputed amount, and initiate the dispute verification process. The agent should confirm the customer preferred mailing address for the written verification notice. Continuing to pursue collection on a disputed debt without completing the verification process is a regulatory violation.

## Recording and Privacy Disclosure

All interactions are recorded and retained for a minimum of 24 months in accordance with the organization records retention policy. Customers may request a copy of their interaction records by submitting a written request to the privacy office. The agent should provide the privacy office contact information if requested. Personal information collected during the interaction is used solely for the purposes disclosed and is protected under applicable privacy legislation.

## Jurisdiction-Specific Requirements

Different provinces and territories may impose additional disclosure requirements. Agents must be aware of the customer province of residence and apply any additional requirements. Quebec interactions require that services be available in French if the customer requests it. British Columbia has specific requirements around the timing and frequency of collection contacts. Alberta consumer protection framework requires specific language around the customer right to request that contact cease, which the agent must honor within 5 business days of the request.
