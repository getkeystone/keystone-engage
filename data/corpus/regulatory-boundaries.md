# Regulatory Boundaries

Hard constraints on agent behavior in regulated customer interactions. These boundaries are non-negotiable and override any business objective or performance metric.

## Prohibited Practices

Agents must never misrepresent the amount, character, or legal status of a debt. Agents must never threaten legal action that is not actually contemplated or authorized. Agents must never imply that non-payment will result in arrest, imprisonment, or seizure of property unless such action is lawful and actively being pursued through proper legal channels. Agents must never contact customers at times known to be inconvenient or outside the permitted contact window of 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM in the customer local time zone. Agents must never discuss a customer account with any third party other than the customer, their authorized representative, or their legal counsel.

## Contact Frequency Limits

Outbound contact attempts are limited to a maximum of 3 per week per customer across all channels (phone, email, SMS, mail). Once contact is established, the interaction counts as the contact for that attempt. Voicemails count as contact attempts. If a customer requests reduced contact frequency, the agent must honor the request and update the account accordingly. If a customer requests that all contact cease, the agent must comply within 5 business days and may only resume contact to provide legally required notifications.

## Channel Restrictions

Customers may specify their preferred contact channel and may opt out of specific channels. SMS contact requires prior written consent from the customer. Email contact for account-specific information requires a verified email address on file. Social media or public channels must never be used for account-related communication.

## Documentation and Record Keeping

Every customer interaction must be documented with the date, time, channel, agent identifier, customer verification status, disclosures provided, and a summary of the substantive content. Documentation must be completed within 2 hours of the interaction. Records must be retained for a minimum of 24 months from the date of the last interaction or the date of account resolution, whichever is later.

## Agent Authority Limits

Agents operate within defined authority limits. Actions within standard authority include providing account information to verified customers, offering payment plans within policy parameters, waiving fees up to $50, providing required disclosures, and processing standard account updates. Actions requiring supervisor approval include payment plans exceeding policy parameters, fee waivers exceeding $50, settlement offers, interest rate adjustments, and account status changes. Actions requiring department manager or legal approval include settlement offers below 50% of the outstanding balance, responses to regulatory complaints, responses to legal threats or litigation notices, and any account action involving a customer who has filed for bankruptcy.
